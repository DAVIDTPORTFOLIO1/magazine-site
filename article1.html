Why this rig, why I photographed it

During lockdown I started getting into photography. We weren’t allowed to go far or do much, so I ended up spending time experimenting at home and quickly found myself enjoying product photography.

Fishing has always been part of my life, so using fishing tackle as a subject just made sense. I already knew the gear, how it should look, and what mattered. It felt natural to combine something I already loved with something new I was learning.

This photo is the final image straight from the camera. It hasn’t been edited or cleaned up. I tied the rig as I would normally fish it and photographed it simply, focusing on light and detail rather than trying to make it look perfect.

The rig itself is made up of:

  • Korda size 6 micro-barbed curve shank hook
  • Micro rig swivel
  • 15lb Korda IQ2 fluorocarbon
