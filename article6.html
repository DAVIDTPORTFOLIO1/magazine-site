Quiet moments on the water

Article

Great Crested Grebe and Chick

This final image shows a great crested grebe heading away from me with one of its young close behind.

The adult bird has a crayfish in her beak, and the youngster is clearly keen to get hold of it. A small wave on the lake happens to obscure the lower part of the birds, which I quite like — it adds to the feeling of movement and distance.

I’d actually just woken up from an afternoon nap (we all have them) when I noticed the grebes and their young right in front of my peg. The adults seemed to be catching something and passing it back and forth.

It was only when I grabbed the camera and put on a long lens — a Sigma 150–600 — that I realised they were feeding on crayfish.

Moments like this are easy to miss, which makes catching them all the more rewarding.
